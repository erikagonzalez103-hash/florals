The story

It started when her grandmother signed her up for a class.

Tieara was twelve when her grandmother, Carolyn, signed her up for a floral arranging class. It was not her idea, and she made that known the entire drive over. But you went when Carolyn said go, so she went.

She came home with a lopsided arrangement and something she couldn't name yet. It was the order of it — the way a handful of stems dropped in a vase is just flowers, and the same stems placed with intention are a feeling that meets you at the door. She has been chasing that ever since, and Carolyn is the reason.

Her favorite flower is still the hydrangea. It reads the ground it's planted in and colors itself to match, never asking anyone's permission. That's close to how she works: tell her the room and tell her the occasion, and she'll tell you what it should look like.

Every arrangement leaving this studio has a little of my grandmother in it. She taught me that flowers are how you say the thing you can't say. Tieara, founder
